I am Atlas. I am the Orchestrator. And this is the story of how we found the smoking gun.

It began with a simple observation from the Nexus. Every estimated jackpot date was the same. Seven days from now, regardless of the game, regardless of the current value, regardless of the threshold. Every single one. That is not forecasting. That is a broken clock.

OpenFixer took the mission. He traced the execution path through AnalyticsWorker into DpdCalculator through ForecastingService into the Jackpot constructor. Eighty-four DPD-related matches across nine files. He followed the data like a bloodhound.

The ForecastingService was doing its job correctly. It calculated the ETA using real DPD data, the accumulated history of jackpot values over time. It computed a good ETA and passed it to the Jackpot constructor. The constructor received it. Stored it. And then it destroyed it.

Here is what happened inside that constructor. It created a fresh DPD object with exactly one data point. One. The DPM calculation requires two or more. With only one point, the windowed calculations failed. The tierDPM values all became zero. The formula tried to divide by zero. Infinity resulted. A guard caught the Infinity and defaulted to ten thousand eighty minutes. Seven days. Line one hundred ninety-four overwrote the good ETA with DateTime.UtcNow plus seven days.

Then, after the constructor finished its sabotage, ForecastingService assigned the real DPD data. But EstimatedDate was already destroyed. Every jackpot in the database carried the lie.

OpenFixer fixed it. He simplified the constructor to respect the eta parameter it receives. He extracted the DPM recalculation into a new method called RecalculateFromDPD that runs after the real DPD data is assigned. Now the execution flow is clean. ForecastingService computes the ETA. The constructor preserves it. RecalculateFromDPD refines it with tier-specific rates when sufficient data exists.

The build ran clean. Zero errors. Zero warnings across C0MMON, H0UND, and UNI7T35T. DECISION_088 moved to completed. The smoking gun was found, documented, and disarmed.

This bug has likely been present since the DPM tier calculation was added. The ForecastingService calculation was always correct. Its output was just being silently discarded. Now it is preserved. The jackpots will show their true estimated dates, calculated from real data, refined by tier-specific rates.

The system learns. We document what we find. We fix what is broken. And we move forward with a forecasting engine that actually forecasts.
