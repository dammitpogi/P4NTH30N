Real GDP (Q3 2025, SAAR): +4.3% (Q2 was +3.8%)  However PCE price index came out +2.8% (Q2: +2.1%) and core PCE even higher at +2.9% (Q2: +2.6%). Gross private domestic investment is roughly flat (-0.02pp), with inventories a drag (-0.22pp). Now, a sizable part of the upside GDP comes from imports falling, which mechanically boosts GDP. A cleaner “underlying demand” read is real final sales to private domestic purchasers at +3.0% that is still strong, but meaningfully below 4.3%. Now, look…a meaningful portion of the headline strength came from trade/import dynamics (imports falling boosts GDP arithmetically), while the underlying domestic-demand proxy was “only” 3.0%. That reduces the case for a clean, broad-based earnings acceleration impulse—so the rates headwind can dominate more easily. SubscribeNarrative matters.
