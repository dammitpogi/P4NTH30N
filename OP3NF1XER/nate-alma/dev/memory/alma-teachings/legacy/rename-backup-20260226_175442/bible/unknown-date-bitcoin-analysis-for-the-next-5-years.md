The main reason is that its fundamentals are not well defined yet, and its trend is much slower than that of the stock market. This may sound strange given the difference in their volatilities, but if you watch BTC, what it mostly does is one big, sharp move toward a target, followed by a multi-week range-bound consolidation there. SubscribeRight now I’m scaling down part of my BTC exposure in my portfolio, because in the future, once it reaches my target level, I want to use those profits to increase Bitcoin’s weight to 10%, maybe even higher. The fundamentals of Bitcoin, together with those of the equity market, are going to change, and that’s why I plan to treat Bitcoin as an investment going forward, rather than as a well-paying, long-term, high-risk speculative trade. The reason is that the stock market is intended to be integrated into the blockchain. When it happens, its risk will still be overestimated.
