Core Concepts

Levels are checkpoints, not predictions

In this doctrine, pivots and targets are used as checkpoints to interpret the market’s internal state. A pivot is a line-in-the-sand: above it, one distribution dominates; below it, another.

Confirmation and invalidation are mandatory

A level is only useful if it changes your behavior. “Confirmation” means the market accepts a scenario. “Invalidation” means it doesn’t. Without this, levels become superstition.

Daily posts as a structured read

A disciplined daily read extracts: event pressure, pivot zones, targets, and the flow/volatility story that explains why those levels matter.

Reverse-engineering beats lagging indicators

The corpus explicitly warns that common retail dashboards (put/call deltas, ratios, and even “order flow” deltas) are lagging data. The alternative method described is to reverse-engineer the structure the market is pricing in, decode hidden patterns (e.g., butterflies and risk-reversal patterns), and derive pivot ranges plus the implied distribution being priced.

Anatomy of a daily post (what to extract)

  1. Premarket OptionsDepth heatmap (context / where liquidity matters).
  2. Script inputs (levels with assigned probabilities).
  3. Speed profile (the expected drift / pressure).
  4. Regime-change flags (vanna flip and “speed slip”).

Expectations vs. behavior (where the edge is)

A practical pattern used throughout the intraday notes is to compare what was expected for the week/day vs how the market actually behaves:

  1. If behavior matches expectation, volatility tends to stay controlled (easy read, even if bearish).
  2. If behavior moves above expectation, sentiment is “out of control,” reinforcing the trend.
  3. If behavior diverges from expectation, it’s the strongest signal (but still needs confirmation).

How To Apply

How to read a daily post (in 5 minutes)

  1. What is the event pressure today/this week?
  2. What is the main pivot range?
  3. What confirms upside vs downside?
  4. Where is invalidation?
  5. What is the expected behavior (suppression, rangebound, trend day)?

Turn levels into a decision (not a prediction)

  1. Pre-commit the invalidation: decide what would make you exit before entry.
  2. Separate targets: treat targets as checkpoints (information), not take-profit fantasies.
  3. Track structure: ask “is the market building structure at the pivot?” not “did it touch?”
  4. Require confirmation: do not treat a bias as a trade until confirmations print.

Common Mistakes

  • Anchoring on one number instead of treating a zone as a zone.
  • Forgetting that the same level can act differently in different regimes.
  • Confusing “support held once” with “support is guaranteed.”
  • Using lagging indicators as if they reveal the current structure.
  • Skipping confirmation/invalidation and calling it “discretion.”

Self-Check

  1. Can you explain what a pivot is in one sentence?
  2. Can you define “downside confirmation” vs “downside target”?
  3. What changes in your plan when volume is low and flows dominate?
