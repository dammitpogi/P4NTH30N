Core Concepts

The archive is your “case law.” It is not the curriculum itself; it is the evidence set. Use it to see how the doctrine is applied under real conditions.

How To Apply

Weekly posts first, intraday posts second

Weekly posts typically frame structure and regime. Intraday posts typically provide timing, confirmations, and concrete levels.

Make your own flashcards

  1. Copy the pivot, targets, and invalidation.
  2. Write the regime state and event pressure.
  3. After the fact, grade process (did confirmations/invalidation work?), not just PnL.

How to read a daily post (outline)

The goal is not to stare at lagging indicators. The goal is to reverse-engineer what structure the market is pricing, then use levels as checkpoints to interpret price action.

  1. Premarket heatmap: get the positioning map (where flows likely matter).
  2. Script inputs: pivots, targets, and probability checkpoints (upside/downside).
  3. Speed profile: the expected drift and whether mechanics are suppressive/supportive.
  4. Vanna flip / speed slip: the regime-change conditions (what breaks the current path).

A disciplined intraday workflow

Most days you only have one true edge opportunity (two at most). The skill is waiting.

  1. Watch the first 30-40 minutes; let the market build and retest a structure.
  2. Define your daily budget: loss limit and profit limit.
  3. Pick one side you understand (long/short) and define entry conditions + size.
  4. Write the invalidation first, take the loss when invalidated, and do not "revenge trade".
  5. If the setup doesn't show up, leave it for tomorrow.

Common Mistakes

  • Reading old posts as “predictions” rather than as examples of a method.
  • Ignoring the event calendar context (CPI/FOMC/OpEx) that made the day behave that way.
  • Cherry-picking only the wins and skipping the disciplined invalidations.
  • Taking every printed level as a signal instead of using levels as checkpoints.
  • Overtrading the open (0DTE noise) instead of waiting for structure.
