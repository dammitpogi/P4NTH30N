About This Textbook

The Doctrine Bible represents a comprehensive compilation of trading knowledge, market insights, and quantitative analysis methods developed over years of daily market observation and research. This curriculum is designed for serious traders who seek to understand not just what moves markets, but why they move.

"Market prices always distort the underlying fundamentals. The degree of distortion can range from negligible to significant. When the influence of the market price on the fundamentals is ignored, we have a misconception about how markets operate." — George Soros, Theory of Reflexivity

The teachings contained herein draw from multiple disciplines: statistical analysis, behavioral finance, options theory, macroeconomics, and geopolitical strategy. Each lesson builds upon the previous, creating a coherent framework for understanding complex market dynamics.

Note for Students

This textbook is best studied sequentially, with each chapter building upon the foundations laid in previous sections. While the Analysis Archive can be referenced at any time, the core curriculum (Chapters I-VI) should be completed in order for maximum comprehension.

How to Use This Textbook

  1. Start with Foundations: Begin with Chapter I to establish the conceptual framework that underlies all subsequent analysis.
  2. Practice Risk Management: Chapter II's principles should be internalized before attempting any live trading.
  3. Study Market Mechanics: Chapter III provides the tools for understanding daily market movements.
  4. Integrate Macro Context: Chapter IV connects micro movements to the broader economic picture.
  5. Apply Technical Skills: Chapter V teaches practical application of the concepts.
  6. Master Asset Classes: Chapter VI provides specialized knowledge for different markets.
  7. Reference the Archive: Chapter VII serves as an ongoing resource for real-world examples.
Key Principles
  • Markets are probabilistic, not deterministic
  • Position sizing matters more than entry timing
  • Volatility is the only true constant
  • Reflexivity creates feedback loops
  • Macro context drives micro movements
  • Risk management preserves capital
  • Continuous learning is essential
